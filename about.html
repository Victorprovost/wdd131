Institute Access Enroll

Institute provides weekday gospel study classes that strengthen faith and testimony in Jesus Christ and His restored gospel. All young single adults ages 18-30 should be encouraged to attend institute classes, whether they are attending school or not.

History

The Institute of Religion program of The Church of Jesus Christ of Latter-day Saints began in 1926 at the University of Idaho, Moscow. It was established to provide religious education and spiritual support for Latter-day Saint students attending colleges and universities. Initially, the program aimed to supplement students' secular education with gospel teachings and to create a community of faith among young adults. Throughout the 20th century, the Institute program expanded significantly, with Institutes being established near major universities across the United States and later internationally. These Institutes offer classes on scripture, Church history, and other gospel topics, providing a place for students to deepen their understanding of the faith and to connect with peers who share their beliefs. Institutes of Religion are often located near campuses, allowing easy access for students. They function under the Church Educational System (CES) and serve as a complement to the Church's seminary program, which focuses on high school students. Institutes also offer various social, cultural, and service activities, helping students to develop spiritually, socially, and academically. Today, the Institute of Religion program continues to play a vital role in the lives of Latter-day Saint young adults, providing education, community, and spiritual enrichment to help them navigate the challenges of higher education and young adulthood.
